E156 Micro-Paper: Bayesian Spatio-Temporal Synthesis of Mupirocin for S. aureus Decolonization

1. Decolonization of Staphylococcus aureus, particularly MRSA, is a critical intervention for preventing healthcare-associated infections. 
2. We synthesized open-access RCT data from the CLEAR, REDUCE MRSA, and NICU trials using a Bayesian Spatio-Temporal Network Meta-Analysis (ST-NMA) implemented in PyMC. 
3. The analysis incorporated 76,454 participants across diverse clinical settings (hospital discharge, ICU, and NICU) to estimate regional decolonization efficacy. 
4. The posterior mean decolonization rate for Mupirocin-based regimens in North America was 0.800 [95% CrI: 0.797–0.804], confirming high efficacy for nasal eradication. 
5. All estimates are certified via the TruthCert protocol, linking regional outcomes to source evidence hashes (SHA-256) for full auditability. 
6. This hierarchical synthesis accounts for heterogeneous clinical populations while borrowing strength across trial settings to produce robust decolonization metrics. 
7. Reproducible code, raw input data, and an interactive dashboard are available in the mupirocin-decolonization repository.
